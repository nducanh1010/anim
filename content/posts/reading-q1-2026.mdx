A short reading list, in the spirit of a commonplace book. One line per
item. If a note runs longer than that, I have not finished thinking
about it yet.

- **The Mushroom at the End of the World**, Anna Tsing. A book about
  collaboration across species, told through matsutake economics.
  Reframes what "value" means when the partners are non-human.
- **A Field Guide to Getting Lost**, Rebecca Solnit. Twenty years old
  and still the right book. The best essay is the first one, but read
  the whole thing anyway.
- **Working in Public**, Nadia Eghbal. The strange new shape of
  maintenance work, told through the lens of open source. The chapter
  on Stripe's reading-list culture is worth the price of admission.
- **The Glass Bead Game**, Hermann Hesse. A friend handed it to me
  with the warning "it gets good around page two hundred." It gets
  good around page two hundred.
- **Pilgrim at Tinker Creek**, Annie Dillard. Re-read every few years.
  Each time a different sentence does the work.
- **A Pattern Language**, Christopher Alexander. Not all of it. The
  first forty patterns, slowly, with a notebook.

I keep the list in a single text file, not an app. Apps are for tasks
I have to do. Reading is not a task. It is a place I go.
